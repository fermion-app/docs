A video is a pre-recorded visual and audio presentation that you can include in your course. Videos can be lectures, tutorials, demonstrations, or any other type of visual content that helps convey information effectively. Videos are engaging and can simplify complex topics through visual representation.

## How do I add videos?

<Steps>

<Step title="Expand course items">
Click the <code>+</code> button to expand the course items' view.<br/>
<img src="https://creator-assets.codedamn.com/fermion-instructor/02-08-2024/instructor_66467ae8ada1f52e23942268/ou2MYg6hixAk6KvqwrmrQ" alt="expand-course-items" />
</Step>

<Step title="Add a Video item">
Click on <code>Video</code>. This will add a video inside your chosen section.<br/>
<img src="https://creator-assets.codedamn.com/fermion-instructor/02-08-2024/instructor_66467ae8ada1f52e23942268/U5CJoCJPUbhR70SvX5_dO" alt="add-video-item" />
</Step>

<Step title="Attach a video">
Click on <code>Attach Video</code> in the newly added <code>Video: Untitled course item</code>.<br/>
<img src="https://creator-assets.codedamn.com/fermion-instructor/02-08-2024/instructor_66467ae8ada1f52e23942268/UEnS7rIrVEAEpquKalIep" alt="attach-video" />
</Step>

<Step title="Edit video details and add a video">
A modal will appear where you can edit the title, add a description, and select <code>Add a video</code>.<br/>
<img src="https://creator-assets.codedamn.com/fermion-instructor/02-08-2024/instructor_66467ae8ada1f52e23942268/o3vfcMD1WYP47Ga1BiRds" alt="video-modal" />
</Step>

<Step title="Select or upload a video from your library">
Click on <code>Add a video</code> to open your <a href="/recorded-videos/video-library">video library</a>. If there are no videos, use the <code>Upload videos</code> button to add videos in bulk.<br/>
<img src="https://creator-assets.codedamn.com/fermion-instructor/02-08-2024/instructor_66467ae8ada1f52e23942268/wr5keXO1dGDtOvu1R1nIW" alt="video-library" />
</Step>

<Step title="Process and link your video">
After uploading, videos may take a few hours to process. Once processed, click <code>Link Video</code> to attach it to your course item.
</Step>

<Step title="Repeat as needed">
You can add multiple pre-recorded videos within your course.
</Step>

</Steps>

## Maximum watch time constraint

The Maximum Watch Time Constraint feature sets a fixed time window for learners to complete watching a recorded video once they begin playback. For example, if the limit is set to 60 minutes,
the learner must finish the video within that duration from the moment they start. After the allotted time expires, access to the video is automatically restricted. This feature is designed to encourage focused, distraction-free learning by ensuring that students watch the video in one sitting rather than pausing or revisiting it later.
